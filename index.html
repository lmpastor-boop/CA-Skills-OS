Project Guide

California Skills Landscape

Start here before a demo. This exhibit maps the current skill composition of California's four key physical-economy sectors, scores each occupation's AI exposure using ONET macro skill data, and projects how skill demand shifts as AI adoption increases across 25 metropolitan areas.

What each tool helps answer
The Scenario Simulator shows how skill shares shift under low and high AI adoption. The Workforce Explorer plots occupations by AI exposure and wage. The Geographic Map shows where sector employment and displacement pressure concentrate across California.
Why California-specific interpretation matters
The same signal looks very different depending on regional labor composition and policy context. Agriculture in the Central Valley, logistics in the Inland Empire, and clean energy across the Bay Area all respond differently to AI adoption pressure.
How to read the skill shares
Each occupation carries a skill profile — the share of its work that falls into Communication, Computers, Information, Machinery, and Management. These shares are employment-weighted to reflect where California's sector workforce actually sits, not what's most common nationally.
Suggested 3-minute walkthrough
Start with the Scenario Simulator — select Transportation and toggle to High Adoption to see the clearest skill shift story. Then open the Geographic Map and switch to Displacement Pressure to see where transition pressure concentrates geographically.
Data sources
BLS OEWS May 2024
Occupation-level employment and wages for all 25 California MSAs. The primary employment data source.
ONET Macro Skills
Five macro skill shares per occupation — Communication, Computers, Information, Machinery, Management.
Scenario modeling
Mechanical displacement model: low (10/8%) and high (45/38%) automation of computers/machinery tasks.
